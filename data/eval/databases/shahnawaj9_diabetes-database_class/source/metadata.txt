&gt; This dataset provides health-related diagnostic records used to predict the likelihood of Type 2 Diabetes. It includes both the **original raw data** and a **cleaned version** where invalid medical entries have been handled. This dual-format approach helps learners and researchers explore data preprocessing, feature engineering, and binary classification modeling in healthcare.

---

### 🔑 Key Features:

- **Dual Format**: Includes both the raw dataset with original values and a cleaned version with imputed medical fields.
- **Medical Focus**: Columns reflect real diagnostic measurements such as glucose levels, BMI, insulin, and blood pressure.
- **Ready for ML**: Cleaned dataset is ideal for training models without extra preprocessing steps.

---

### 🧾 Attributes:

- **Pregnancies**: Number of times pregnant  
- **Glucose**: Plasma glucose concentration  
- **BloodPressure**: Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg)  
- **SkinThickness**: Triceps skin fold thickness (mm)  
- **Insulin**: 2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml)  
- **BMI**: Body mass index (weight in kg / height in m²)  
- **DiabetesPedigreeFunction**: Likelihood of diabetes based on family history  
- **Age**: Age in years  
- **Outcome**: Target variable (1 = diabetic, 0 = non-diabetic)

---

### ⚙️ Data Processing:

The raw dataset contains 0s in certain columns where zero values are medically impossible (e.g., Glucose, Insulin, BMI). These 0s were replaced with `NaN`, and then imputed using **median values** to produce the cleaned dataset. This process ensures better model performance and more reliable health predictions.

---

### 💡 Applications:

- **Diabetes Risk Prediction**: Train ML models to predict diabetes onset.
- **Healthcare Modeling**: Explore trends in patient diagnostics and health outcomes.
- **Feature Engineering Practice**: Use both raw and cleaned versions to demonstrate preprocessing workflows.

---
