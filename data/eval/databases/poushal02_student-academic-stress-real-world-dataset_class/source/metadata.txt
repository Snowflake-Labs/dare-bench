## 📄 **Dataset: Academic Stress Analysis Among Students**

### **Overview**
This dataset captures **real-world insights into the academic stress levels of students** across different educational stages. The data was collected via **Google Forms** from a diverse group of students spanning **high school, undergraduate, and postgraduate** levels. 

You can give your data by filling this form and help this dataset grow :
https://forms.gle/awqySh98RqENkmVc9

Academic stress is a growing concern among students worldwide, significantly affecting their mental well-being and academic performance. This dataset aims to quantify the factors contributing to academic stress and provide a foundation for **data-driven research, mental health analysis, and educational policy improvement**.

***

### 🧩 **Data Collection**
- **Source:** Structured Google Form survey  
- **Population:** Students from various institutions, academic stages, and disciplines  
- **Sampling Method:** Voluntary participation through social media groups, student communities, and direct outreach

### 📊 **Dataset Structure**
The dataset maintains a **tabular format** with 8 columns:  

| Column Name | Data Type | Description |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Academic Stage** | Categorical (`high school`, `undergraduate`, `postgraduate`) | Educational level of the student, useful for analyzing stress differences by stage. |
| **Peer Pressure** | Numerical (1–5 scale) | Influence of peers through competition, comparison, and social expectations — a notable driver of stress. |
| **Academic Pressure from Home** | Numerical (1–5 scale) | Extent of academic expectations or pressure exerted by family or household. |
| **Study Environment** | Categorical (`noisy`, `peaceful`, `disrupted`) | Quality of the study environment impacting focus and productivity. |
| **Coping Strategy** | Categorical (`analyzing the problem with intellect`, `emotional breakdown`, `social support`) | Methods students use to cope with stress and challenges. |
| **Bad Habits** | Text / Multi-category (e.g., `smoking`, `drinking`, `none`) | Negative habits students may resort to under stress. |
| **Academic Competition** | Numerical (1–5 scale) | Intensity of competition among peers, which can elevate stress levels. |
| **Stress Index** *(Target Variable)* | Numerical (1–5 scale) | Self-reported stress level of the student, serving as the dependent variable for prediction. |

***
## **📟 Data Feature details**
**Academic Stage** : This feature has the values high school, undergraduate, postgraduate. The academic stage can be an effective information on this data.

**Peer Pressure** : This feature is the scaling from 1 to 5. Peer pressure on the sense of jealousy, competition, comparison, etc factors that highly impact the student's mental health. In many cases students feel less confident, or stressful reasoned by these matters.

**Academic Pressure from your home** : This feature is the scaling from 1 to 5. In our society the pressure from household effects negatively in the academic life of students. In most scenarios the reasons behind the cases of suicides of students are found in the household environment of students.

**Study Environment** : This feature has the choices - noisy, peaceful, disrupted. The peaceful, organized study desk brings the most productive study methods, and creates an organized mindset for the student. On the contrary, the noisy, disrupted desk often ruins the academic mentality of student, increasing stress.

**Coping strategy students use** : This feature has the values - analyzing the problem with intellect, the emotional breakdown, or social support. The reason behind this topic is to identify the source of emotional intelligence of student, which reversibly impacts the stress of the student.

**Bad Habits** : This feature is included to get the student's stress relief methodology. The more the student lean on bad habits like smoking, drinking, or etc the less likely their stress is going to release.

**Academic Competition** : This feature has one goal, to get the academic competition among students, which largely increases stress.

**Stress Index** : The output column taken from the student on a scale of 1 to 5.

### 🎯 **Purpose & Goals**
The primary goal of this dataset is to **analyze trends, factors, and correlations** contributing to academic stress. It can be used for:
- **Mental Health Studies** – Understanding behavioral and environmental triggers of stress.
- **Educational Research** – Building models to predict stress levels and recommending interventions.
- **Policy Making** – Designing informed programs to reduce student stress.
- **Machine Learning Projects** – Stress prediction, regression modeling, clustering for identifying stress profiles.

***

### 💡 **Potential Use Cases**
- **Stress Level Prediction:** Train ML models to predict stress index using academic and environmental factors.
- **Correlation Analysis:** Study relationships between peer pressure, academic competition, and mental well-being.
- **Cluster Analysis:** Group students into stress categories to design tailored support strategies.
- **Data Visualization Projects:** Create dashboards communicating student well-being patterns.

***

### ⚠️ **Ethical Considerations**
- All responses were collected **anonymously** with participant consent.
- The dataset should not be used for profiling or making harmful assumptions about specific individuals or groups.
- When building predictive models, be mindful of **bias** and the **context of the results**.
