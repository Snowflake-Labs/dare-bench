# Linear Performance Pricing (LPP) Pipe Pricing Dataset

This synthetic dataset simulates **supplier quotes for iron pipes** of varying lengths. It is designed to demonstrate **Linear Performance Pricing (LPP)**, a procurement analytics technique used to identify cost-saving opportunities by correlating product prices with performance parameters (e.g., pipe length). The dataset includes:
- **Real-world variations**: Noise, outliers, and multiple suppliers.
- **Target prices**: Calculated using market trends (`target_price_market`) and best-practice benchmarks (`target_price_benchmark`).

Inspired by the example from "Data-Driven Spend Management" (Chapter 3).
## **Suggested Analysis Tasks**
1. **Regression Analysis**: Replicate the market line (`P^M = 1.465 + 1.076L`) using linear regression.  
2. **Outlier Detection**: Identify overpriced quotes using Z-scores or IQR.  
3. **Savings Calculation**: Compute total savings if prices are negotiated down to the benchmark.  
4. **Supplier Comparison**: Analyze pricing strategies of S1 vs S2 vs S3 vs S4.  
5. **Visualization**: Plot price vs. length with market/benchmark lines.

=== About this file ===

About this file
Synthetic iron pipe pricing dataset for Linear Performance Pricing (LPP) analysis, featuring pipe lengths, supplier quotes, and target prices. Ideal for regression modeling, outlier detection, and procurement cost-saving strategies in supply chain analytics.


=== Columns & descriptions ===

pipe_id: Unique identifier for each pipe quote.
pipe_length_m: Length of the pipe in meters (performance parameter).
price_eur: Quoted price of the pipe in Euros (simulated with noise).
supplier_id: Supplier ID (S1, S2, S3, S4).
is_outlier: `True` if the quote is an artificially inflated outlier
target_price_market: Market price calculated via regression.
target_price_benchmark: Best-practice benchmark price.
negotiation_room: Potential savings.
